# DOMAIN.md — TidePool

**Charter:** the human/domain home — tidepooling knowledge the code encodes.

## Tides
- A good tidepooling window is a **daylight low at or below 0.0 ft MLLW**; below −1.0 ft is
  exceptional ("minus tide").
- Spring tides (new/full moon) have the largest range — the best lows cluster around syzygy;
  neap tides (quarters) rarely produce workable windows.
- Arrive ~1 h before the predicted low; the window closes fast on the flood.

## East vs West coast
- East-coast (semidiurnal) stations run two similar lows per day, often pre-dawn; West-coast
  mixed tides give one deep low — the deep low is the plannable one.

## Safety (encoded as scoring floors, not suggestions)
- Never plan a window that ends after sunset — the daylight factor is a hard 0 outside
  sunrise→sunset, not a discount.
- Swell overrides score: a 15-ft WNW swell makes a −1.5 ft low unusable (future NDBC factor).
